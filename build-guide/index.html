    Build Guide — L-Shaped Raised Bed Construction¶

    Home | Layout | Construction | Build Guide | Materials | Soil | Drainage | Checklist | Winterberry | Azalea | Bluebells | Phlox

    This guide provides the step-by-step sequence for constructing the L-shaped raised bed on a concrete patio using Belgard DESIGNFORMS Scandina Grey panels.

    1. Materials & Tools¶

    Shopping List¶

    • Wall Panels: ~25 Large panels (12"x24"x2.6") for 2 courses (24" height).
    • Cap Panels: ~11 Small panels (6"x24"x2.6") for a concrete cap ledge.
    • Hardware: 3–4 Belgard DESIGNFORMS Hardware Kits (includes brackets, connectors, screws, nuts).
    • Drainage: ~7 cu ft of 3/4" washed gravel (limestone-free), ~21 sq ft of 1/2" mesh galvanized hardware cloth, ~21 sq ft of non-woven geotextile fabric.
    • Piping: 3" perforated PVC pipe (length for base grid).
    • pH Barrier: 6–12" strips of rigid HDPE root barrier or cedar board.
    • Soil Components: ~17 cu ft aged blended compost, ~17 cu ft pine bark fines/peat/perlite mix, ~7 cu ft loamy topsoil.
    • Amendments: Elemental sulfur (for acid zone).

    Tools¶

    • Power drill with 1/4" socket adaptor.
    • 7/16" hex socket (minimum 1-3/4" deep).
    • 1–1.5" masonry/hammer drill bit.
    • Masonry saw or angle grinder with diamond blade (for panel cuts).
    • Penetrating concrete sealer (Siloxa-Tek or RadonSeal).

    2. Site Prep¶

    1. Clean & Seal: Pressure wash the patio footprint. Apply penetrating concrete sealer to the bed area to prevent staining and lime leaching.
    2. Layout Marking: Mark the interior footprint on the concrete.
    3. Long side: 60" x 30"
    4. Short side: 18" x 24"
    5. Air Gap: Place 1/2" spacer blocks or bed feet along the perimeter to allow lateral water flow under the frame.

    3. Wall Assembly¶

    Assemble the L-shape using the DESIGNFORMS hardware system. Each course is 12" tall; use 2 courses for a 24" total height.

    Assembly Steps¶

    1. Base Course: Lay 11 panels per course.
    2. Back: 3 panels (cut one to 12").
    3. Right (Long): 2 panels (cut one to 6").
    4. Step: 2 panels (cut one to 12").
    5. Right (Short): 1 panel (cut to 18").
    6. Bottom: 1 panel (24").
    7. Left: 2 panels (48").
    8. Fasten: Use joining brackets for straight runs and connectors for corners. Secure with screws and nuts using the 7/16" socket.
    9. Second Course: Repeat the layout, staggering joints where possible for stability.
    10. Cap: Lay small panels flat on top of the wall as a ledge.

    See Raised Bed Construction for joining details.

    4. Drainage Layer¶

    1. Hardware Cloth: Install 1/2" mesh galvanized hardware cloth as a basket inside the frame, securing it to the inner walls.
    2. Weep Holes: Drill 1–1.5" holes through the panels 1–2" above the base, every 12–16" around the perimeter.
    3. Gravel & Pipe: Add a 3–4" layer of 3/4" washed gravel. Embed 3" perforated PVC pipe in the gravel, routing it toward the weep holes.
    4. Fabric: Layer non-woven geotextile fabric over the gravel to prevent soil migration.

    See Drainage Engineering for the full analysis.

    5. pH Barrier¶

    1. Location: Install the barrier at the junction between the long leg (Acid Zone) and short leg (Neutral Zone).
    2. Installation: Stand 6–12" strips of HDPE or cedar board vertically. Ensure it spans the full soil column depth (16–18") above the drainage layer.

    6. Soil Fill¶

    Fill the two zones with their respective recipes. Total soil volume is ~34 cu ft after the drainage layer.

    Acid Zone (Long Leg — ~21 cu ft)¶

    • 35% aged blended compost.
    • 25% pine bark fines (≤1/4").
    • 20% peat moss.
    • 20% perlite or horticultural grit.
    • Mix in elemental sulfur to target pH 4.5–5.5.

    Neutral Zone (Short Leg — ~13 cu ft)¶

    • 40% loamy topsoil.
    • 25% aged compost.
    • 20% coarse grit or pea gravel.
    • 15% coarse perlite.
    • Phlox Sub-Zone: At the outer edge, increase grit to 30% and add 10% coarse sand for rapid drainage.

    See Soil Composition for mix details.

    7. Planting¶

    1. Acid Zone:
    2. Winterberry 'Winter Red': Center-back position for full sun.
    3. Azalea 'Mandarin Lights' (x2): Flanking the winterberry.
    4. Neutral Zone:
    5. Virginia Bluebells (x2): Shaded side of the bed.
    6. Snowflake Carpet Phlox: Outer edge to cascade over the wall.

    See Bed Layout & Compatibility for spacing and sun requirements.

    8. Mulch & Finish¶

    1. Mulch:
    2. Acid Zone: 2–3" of pine bark or pine needles.
    3. Neutral Zone: Leaf mulch over bluebells.
    4. Warning: Do not mulch the crown of the phlox.
    5. Watering: Perform a deep initial watering. Ensure the acid zone is thoroughly saturated.
    6. Final Check: Verify weep holes are clear and water flows freely from the base.

This calendar consolidates recurring care tasks for the L-shaped raised bed in Zone 7b-8a. It covers Winter Red winterberry, Mandarin Lights azalea, Virginia bluebells, and Snowflake carpet phlox, along with general soil and drainage maintenance.

## Quick Reference: Seasonal Watering

| Plant | Spring (Mar–May) | Summer (Jun–Aug) | Fall (Sep–Nov) | Winter (Dec–Feb) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Winterberry** | 2x/week | 2–3x/week | 1–2x/week | Every 2–3 weeks* |
| **Azalea** | Every 5–7 days | Every 2–3 days | Every 10–14 days | Monthly* |
| **Bluebells** | 1–3x/week** | **NONE** | None | None |
| **Phlox** | Every 5–7 days | Every 3–5 days | Every 10–14 days | Every 3–4 weeks* |

\* *Only if no significant rain; do not let roots freeze-dry in the raised bed.*
\** *Taper off in May as foliage declines; stop completely once dormant.*

---

## January
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Monitor berries; birds may begin feeding. Water lightly if soil is dry.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Ensure 2–3" mulch is intact. No watering unless prolonged drought.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Check that location markers are visible so crowns aren't disturbed.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Semi-evergreen foliage persists; no maintenance needed.
- [ ] **General:** Inspect weep holes for clogs from winter debris or ice.

## February
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Prune for shaping if needed before buds swell. Remove root suckers at ground level.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Apply Holly-Tone or acid-specific fertilizer as flower buds swell.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Watch for early sprouts in late February. Keep soil moist.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Apply slow-release balanced fertilizer (10-10-10). Trim any brown, winter-damaged areas.
- [ ] **General:** Test soil pH in both Acid and Neutral zones.

## March
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Apply Holly-Tone as buds swell. Remove any new root suckers.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Peak bloom begins. Maintain consistent moisture ("damp sponge" texture).
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Active growth phase. Water 2–3x/week to support vigorous shoots.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Peak bloom begins. Water regularly if the top inch of soil is dry.
- [ ] **General:** Apply elemental sulfur to the Acid Zone if pH has drifted above 5.5.

## April
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Monitor for leafminers as leaves emerge. Apply 2–3" acidic mulch (pine bark/needles).
- [ ] **Azalea:** Peak bloom. Prune immediately after flowers fade. Deadhead spent clusters.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Peak bloom. Reduce watering slightly (1–2x/week) during flowering.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Peak bloom. Ensure full sun exposure is not blocked by new growth of neighbors.
- [ ] **General:** Check drainage performance after heavy spring rains; ensure no pooling at the bed base.

## May
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Flowering begins. Avoid all pesticides to protect pollinators for berry set.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Main annual feeding with Holly-Tone. Monitor for lace bugs on leaf undersides.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Foliage will yellow and collapse. Taper watering to zero by month's end.
- [ ] **Phlox:** **Shear the entire mat back by 1/3 to 1/2** immediately after the final flowers fade.
- [ ] **General:** Monitor for early pest activity (leafminers, lace bugs, aphids).

## June
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Green berries forming. Keep soil consistently moist. Top-dress mulch if needed.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Active growth. Final deadline for fertilizer is July 1. Monitor for lace bugs.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** **Fully dormant.** Do not water this zone. Do not dig or disturb the soil.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Water every 3–5 days. Check for spider mites if weather is hot and dry.
- [ ] **General:** Check weep holes; ensure water exits freely during summer storms.

## July
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Berries swelling. Water deeply 2–3x/week. No fertilizer during peak heat.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Heat stress phase. Water every 2–3 days. Morning-only irrigation to prevent disease.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Dormant. Ensure nearby watering does not saturate the bluebell root zone.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Water every 3–5 days. Monitor for spider mites (bronze leaves, fine webbing).
- [ ] **General:** Maintain 2–3" mulch depth to insulate roots from concrete-radiated heat.

## August
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Critical moisture month. Drought stress now causes premature fruit drop.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Monitor for lace bugs and spider mites. No fertilizer.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Dormant. No maintenance.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Water regularly. Check for center die-out; plan for September division if needed.
- [ ] **General:** Inspect plants for signs of heat stress or drought (scorch, wilting, fruit drop).

## September
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Berries begin turning red. Optional light feeding with Holly-Tone (early Sept only).
- [ ] **Azalea:** Reduce watering frequency to help the plant harden off for winter. No fertilizer.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Ideal time to divide established clumps or add new dormant corms.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Divide every 2–3 years to maintain a dense mat. Reduce watering as temps cool.
- [ ] **General:** Top off soil levels if settling has occurred (expect ~15% volume loss in year 1).

## October
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Leaves drop, exposing red berries. Peak ornamental display.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Apply fresh 2–3" acidic mulch. Pull mulch 5–6" away from the main stems.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Apply 2–3" leaf mulch over the planting area to mimic forest floor conditions.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Semi-evergreen foliage remains green. No maintenance needed.
- [ ] **General:** Clear fallen leaves from weep holes and the patio surface around the bed.

## November
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Berries persist. Cut stems for holiday arrangements (this serves as light pruning).
- [ ] **Azalea:** Give one final deep watering after the first hard frost if the fall was dry.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Fully dormant.
- [ ] **Phlox:** No maintenance.
- [ ] **General:** Ensure all drainage outlets are clear before winter freezes.

## December
- [ ] **Winterberry:** Berries persist. Water every 2–3 weeks if there is no rain or snow.
- [ ] **Azalea:** Fully dormant. Monitor mulch depth.
- [ ] **Bluebells:** Fully dormant.
- [ ] **Phlox:** Semi-evergreen foliage provides winter texture.
- [ ] **General:** Monitor for "freeze-drying" in the raised bed; water lightly during warm winter spells.

## Signs of Trouble

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yellow leaves, green veins** | High soil pH (Chlorosis) | Test pH; apply elemental sulfur or chelated iron. |
| **Wilting in morning (soil moist)** | Root Rot (Phytophthora) | Improve drainage; reduce watering; remove dead wood. |
| **Berries dropping early** | Summer drought stress | Increase watering to 2–3x/week; check mulch depth. |
| **No berries on mature plant** | Lack of pollination | Ensure 'Southern Gentleman' male is within 50 feet. |
| **Bronze leaves, fine webbing** | Spider Mites | Spray leaf undersides with strong water jet or soap. |
| **Yellow stippling on leaves** | Lace Bugs | Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil to leaf undersides. |
| **White powdery coating** | Powdery Mildew | Improve air circulation; apply copper soap fungicide. |
| **Brown, wedge-shaped leaf patches** | Foliar Nematodes | Remove and destroy affected plant; do not compost. |
| **Leaf notches on edges** | Weevils | Apply entomopathogenic nematodes to soil. |
| **Bare/woody center in mat** | Aging or skipped pruning | Divide plant in fall; ensure annual post-bloom shearing. |
